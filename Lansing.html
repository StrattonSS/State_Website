Welcome to Lansing, Michigan

Lansing, Michigan’s dynamic capital, offers a unique fusion of history, progress, and Midwestern charm. Home to the majestic State Capitol and Michigan State University, Lansing pulses with civic pride and educational energy. Stroll along the scenic River Trail or explore colorful art districts filled with murals, galleries, and indie shops. Whether you’re catching a game, enjoying the city’s growing food and brewery scene, or relaxing under a canopy of tree-lined streets, Lansing strikes a perfect balance between innovation and nature—welcoming residents and visitors alike with its approachable, community-driven spirit.

Top 5 Things to Do in Lansing

  1. Tour the historic Michigan State Capitol building.
  2. Explore Impression 5 Science Center with the family.
  3. Stroll through Potter Park Zoo and enjoy wildlife exhibits.
  4. Catch a Lansing Lugnuts game at Jackson Field.
  5. Walk or bike the scenic Lansing River Trail.

Did you know?

  • The Michigan State Capitol was one of the first buildings in the U.S. to feature a cast-iron dome.
  • Lansing was named the capital in 1847, largely for its central location and to encourage settlement inland.
  • The Grand River, which runs through Lansing, is the longest river entirely within Michigan.
