About Michigan

Nestled in the heart of the Great Lakes region, Michigan is the only state in the U.S. made up of two separate peninsulas — the Upper and Lower — connected by the majestic Mackinac Bridge. Surrounded by freshwater on nearly every side, Michigan offers a one-of-a-kind landscape where forests, lakes, and vibrant cities coexist in perfect balance.

The state's economic history is deeply rooted in innovation and industry. From the birthplace of the American auto industry in Detroit to expansive farmland and bustling tourism hubs, Michigan continues to evolve while honoring its proud manufacturing legacy.

But Michigan isn’t just machines and motors — it’s also music, museums, and miles of trails. Whether you’re exploring the artisan cafés of Ann Arbor, catching live music in Grand Rapids, or wandering the historic streets of Lansing, Michigan offers a unique blend of Midwestern charm and forward-thinking energy.

Discover Michigan’s Natural Wonders

Michigan is a paradise for nature lovers. Surrounded by four of the five Great Lakes, the state boasts over 3,000 miles of freshwater shoreline — more than any other state in the U.S. Whether you're strolling a sandy beach or watching the waves crash on rocky bluffs, the lakes offer breathtaking views year-round.

Along the shores of Lake Superior, you’ll find the Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore — a spectacular stretch of colorful sandstone cliffs, hidden coves, and crystal-clear waters perfect for kayaking and hiking. It’s one of the crown jewels of the Upper Peninsula.

Further inland, Michigan’s natural beauty continues with the massive dunes of Sleeping Bear and Silver Lake, where rolling hills of golden sand meet forest and shoreline. And don't miss the hundreds of waterfalls scattered across the Upper Peninsula, from the iconic Tahquamenon Falls to serene hidden cascades tucked deep in the woods.

Whether you're exploring coastal cliffs, forested trails, or cascading falls, Michigan’s landscapes offer an unforgettable adventure in every season.

Did you know?

  • Michigan is the only state that consists of two peninsulas.
  • It's home to the world's largest freshwater sand dunes, located in Silver Lake State Park.
  • Michigan has more than 150 lighthouses—more than any other U.S. state.
